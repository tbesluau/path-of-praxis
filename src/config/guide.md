## Actions & Action Levels

Every character performs actions during battle — either weapon attacks or spells. Each action has its own level that rises as you use it.

Higher action levels increase the effectiveness of that action. You start with a few actions unlocked; more become available as your action levels grow.

## Action Experience & Speed

Actions gain experience as you use them in battle. The rate at which experience accumulates depends on enemy level and the nature of the action itself.

Focusing on one action concentrates experience into it and levels it quickly. Spreading across many actions develops a broader toolkit, but each one levels more slowly.

## Life & Mana

**Life** is your health. Reaching zero ends the run. Life recovers between battles.

**Mana** is spent to cast spells. It regenerates over time. Running low on mana limits how frequently you can cast, so balance spell use with recovery.

Both have a maximum value that increases as their levels rise.

## Life & Mana Experience & Speed

Life and mana each have their own level and experience track, independent of actions. Life experience is gained by surviving battles and taking damage. Mana experience is gained by spending mana on spells.

Levelling life increases your maximum life. Levelling mana increases your maximum mana. Experience speed scales with how actively you engage each resource — taking more hits or casting more spells accelerates growth.

## Death & Rebirth

When your life reaches zero, your run ends. All experience gained during the run — actions, life, mana, and masteries — is applied to your character permanently before the run resets.

Rebirth returns you to the beginning with your accumulated progression intact. Each run makes your character meaningfully stronger, regardless of how far you got.

## Masteries

Masteries are long-term passive upgrades that persist across all runs. Each mastery has its own experience track that fills through related actions and activities.

When a mastery levels up you earn a mastery point. Open a mastery's skill tree to spend points on permanent passive bonuses. Trees contain small, strong, and major nodes in sequence, with special key nodes branching off each major node.

Unspent mastery points are never lost — they carry over between runs.
